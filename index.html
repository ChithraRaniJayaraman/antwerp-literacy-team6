Vision of this Blueprint

We envision a district where every student—regardless of home language or background—leaves secondary school as a confident, capable reader.

In this vision, reading is not confined to language classes. It happens across all subjects: students in science class can interpret complex texts, students in history can analyze primary sources, and students in vocational tracks can read and follow technical instructions independently. Reading becomes the foundation upon which all other learning is built.

Teachers in every discipline see themselves as reading teachers. They have the training, the tools, and the time to support students' comprehension. They collaborate across subjects, ensuring that reading strategies are reinforced consistently throughout the school day.

Students take ownership of their own reading development. They set goals, track their progress, and reflect on what strategies work for them. They read not only because they have to, but because they find meaning and interest in what texts offer.

Families are partners in this work. They understand how to support reading at home, even when Dutch is not their first language. They feel connected to the school and confident in their role.

This is what we are working toward: a district where strong reading literacy is not an exception, but the expectation—for every student, in every school, every day.

Context

Our client is a school district in the Antwerp region, serving nine secondary schools and approximately 8,500 students. The district includes seven general secondary schools and two schools for special educational needs.

The student population is highly diverse. Many students come from Turkish, Moroccan, and Eastern European backgrounds, and a significant number speak a language other than Dutch at home. This linguistic diversity is a strength, but it also means many students are learning to read in a language that is not their first.

Teachers across subjects report that reading comprehension struggles affect learning in science, history, and beyond. Yet most have not been trained to teach reading—especially in multilingual classrooms. Families want to help but often face language barriers themselves.

The district leadership recognizes this challenge and is committed to a long-term, coordinated approach. This blueprint is designed to meet that need.
