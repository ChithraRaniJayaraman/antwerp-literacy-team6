Vision of this Blueprint

We envision a district where every student—regardless of home language or background—leaves secondary school as a confident, capable reader.

In this vision, reading is not confined to language classes. It happens across all subjects: students in science class can interpret complex texts, students in history can analyze primary sources, and students in vocational tracks can read and follow technical instructions independently.

Teachers in every discipline see themselves as reading teachers. They have the training, the tools, and the time to support students' comprehension. They collaborate across subjects, ensuring that reading strategies are reinforced consistently throughout the school day.

Students take ownership of their own reading development. They set goals, track their progress, and reflect on what strategies work for them. Families are partners in this work and feel connected to the school and confident in their role.

Context

Our client is a school district in the Antwerp region, serving nine secondary schools and approximately 8,500 students. The district includes seven general secondary schools and two schools for special educational needs.

The student population is highly diverse. Many students come from Turkish, Moroccan, and Eastern European backgrounds, and a significant number speak a language other than Dutch at home. This linguistic diversity is a strength, but it also means many students are learning to read in a language that is not their first.

Teachers across subjects report that reading comprehension struggles affect learning in science, history, and beyond. Families want to help but often face language barriers themselves.

Vision & Direction

Peer Tutoring

Students support each other through structured reading partnerships that build confidence, comprehension, and communication skills.

Parental Engagement

Families are included as partners in literacy development through accessible communication, support tools, and school connection.

Self-Regulated Learning

Students learn to set goals, monitor progress, reflect on strategies, and take ownership of their reading development.

School-wide Impact

Reading becomes a shared responsibility across subjects, helping students succeed in every classroom and every school day.

Strategic Priorities

Reading Across Subjects

Support teachers in every discipline to include reading strategies that improve understanding and learning outcomes.

Family-School Connection

Strengthen communication and trust with families so literacy support continues both inside and outside school.

Student Ownership

Help learners build independence through reflection, goal setting, and active engagement with meaningful texts.

Long-term Sustainability

Create a coordinated and lasting literacy model that can be shared across schools in the Antwerp region.
